Editor's Note:

This edition of the classic American novel Little Women, originally published in 1868 (now on public record) and written by Louisa May Alcott, integrates text, imagery, audio, and music from 2019 Academy Award nominated film adaptation by Greta Gerwig.

The current redesign of this literary work, done on HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, aspires to appeal to diverse readers and consumers of this product who want to receive the content through various mediums. This project becomes timely as the novel and recent film adaptation speak to women's issues that are still present today. Also, many of the audiences of the films would want to read the novel in a way that speaks to them, which might have to do with the actors or the music.

The colors have been chosen to represent the colors of the original cover. The pdf for the 1868 edition of the novel can be foundhere.

The March sisters are the center of the story, not any of the male counterparts, so their faces are highlighted from the start of the page. Alcott wrote this almost as a reflection of herself in Jo, the main character.
